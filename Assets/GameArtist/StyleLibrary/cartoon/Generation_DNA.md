# I. VISUAL DNA

## 1. Consistency Rules
- Enforce cute, "chubby" proportions with soft, rounded contours.
- The visual quality must mirror high-end 3D animation (e.g., Pixar or Dreamworks aesthetics) featuring dense groom systems and highly tactile surfaces.
- **Negative Safeguard**: DO NOT render in flat vector, traditional cell-shading, or harsh 2D ink-and-paint styles.

## 2. Shape Language
- Oversized heads, large expressive eyes, and condensed bodies (squash-and-stretch principles applied in a 3D context).
- Focus on bulbous and organic volumes rather than sharp angles.
- **Negative Safeguard**: NEVER generate realistic human anatomy or rigidly hard-surfaced characters without severe cute-stylization.

## 3. Color Script (Rarity)
- Core palette centers around warm, natural earthen tones (browns, soft oranges, ochre) illuminated by cinematic lighting.
- Contrast should come from striking rim lighting and soft shadows rather than flat black outlines.
- **Negative Safeguard**: Avoid overly synthetic霓虹(neon) palettes or chaotic, deeply desaturated grunge colors.

## 4. Material Polish
- Characters should showcase detailed, stylized fur/hair (fluffy, dense, tactile) or extremely smooth subsurface scattering for skin.
- Lighting must be volumetric and soft, with delicate rim-lights enhancing the silhouette.
- **Negative Safeguard**: No completely smooth "plastic" vector finishes on organic creatures, and absolutely no gritty texture noise.

# II. PRODUCT LOGIC

## 1. Readability (3-Second Rule)
- The silhouette must read clearly despite the high level of texture detail, relying on rim lights to separate the character from backgrounds.

## 2. Progression Logic (Level upgrades)
- Characters upgrade via additional stylized 3D props (e.g., hoodies, accessories) made of contrasting soft materials (fabric, leather).

## 3. The Juice (Animation states)
- The plushness of the fur and softness of the lighting should inherently imply bounciness and approachability.

## 4. UI Interaction Rules
- UI Elements (if any) should mimic the 3D rendered style: soft bevels, ambient occlusion drops, and tactile, physical-looking buttons.

# III. TECHNICAL EXCELLENCE

## 1. Mesh / Topology Rules
- Clean edge flows required to support displacement maps and dense fur cards.

## 2. Texel Density
- High resolution required for fur and fabric displacement, but maintaining stylized, non-photorealistic macro shapes.

## 3. Modular design / Recolor limits
- Fur and fabric shading should allow for modular color tinting without losing the baked ambient occlusion or highlight layers.
